About RoboCupJunior

RoboCupJunior is an international educational robotics initiative for young students, encouraging learning through robotics, programming, teamwork, and innovation.

About RoboCup

The RoboCup organization is run by a dedicated team of volunteers, researchers, professionals, and more worldwide. RoboCup provides opportunities for students from age 10 to 19 in Junior leagues and students from age 19+ in a Major division.

What is RoboCupJunior?

RoboCupJunior (RCJ) is a project-oriented educational initiative that sponsors local, regional and international robotic events for young students. It is designed to introduce robotics learning in an interdisciplinary approach for primary and secondary students.

RCJ stands apart from other robotics initiatives for its international collaboration with a strong community of practice in robotics skills worldwide since the year 2000.

Why RoboCupJunior?

Focused on Education

RCJ is focused on education and gives students the opportunity to learn robotics in a supportive, project-based environment.

Scaffolded Learning

RCJ challenges remain the same from one year to the next, providing a scaffolded learning environment where students can develop more sophisticated solutions as they grow and expand their knowledge.

Familiar Leagues

RCJ leagues — OnStage, Rescue and Soccer — are familiar, allowing spectators to watch and understand what they are observing without needing explanations of complicated rules.

Computer Science

RCJ delves more deeply into computer science and programming because of its emphasis on autonomous robots.

International RoboCup

RCJ sits at the entry-level of the international RoboCup initiative, which is strongly committed to education and involvement of young people in technology.

Global Community

RoboCupJunior connects students, mentors, and educators through an international robotics community that has been active since 2000.

RoboCupJunior Community

RoboCupJunior has a strong international community where students, mentors, educators, and organizers can share knowledge and resources.
