Rescue LineRules

Artificial intelligence at its best: Rescue mirrors real-life robot missions that rescue people from life-threatening situations.

Back to Leagues →

Field Zones

Line Follow Zone

  • Navigate a black line on white tiles.
  • Follow specific directional indicators at intersections, which may lead to dead ends if not followed.
  • Navigate around obstacles.
  • Handle possible gaps in the path.
  • Handle inclines such as a seesaw or ramp.
  • Overcome speed bumps.

Evacuation Zone

  • Represents a war zone or natural disaster site where victims need to be rescued.
  • The robot brings victims into the evacuation point.
  • The evacuation point is a black right-angle triangle with 30 cm x 30 cm sides.
  • Level 1 uses a 5 cm bump along the side that does not touch the wall.
  • Level 2 uses a 6 cm bump along the side that does not touch the wall.

Scoring, Checkpoints & Victims

Line Follow Points

  • 15 points for the correct path at an intersection.
  • 10 points for overcoming obstacles.
  • 10 points for reacquiring the line after a gap.
  • 5 points per tile on the 1st attempt.
  • 3 points per tile on the 2nd attempt.
  • 1 point per tile on the 3rd attempt.
  • 0 points per tile after the 3rd attempt.
  • 5 points for speed bumps and inclines.

Checkpoints & Lack of Progress

  • A checkpoint is a tile where a robot is manually placed back when lack of progress occurs.
  • Checkpoints are not located on tiles with scoring elements.
  • The start tile is a checkpoint where the robot can restart.
  • A checkpoint marker indicates checkpoint tiles.
  • An attempt is each time the robot is moved back to a checkpoint.
  • Lack of progress can be declared by the team captain.
  • Lack of progress also occurs when the robot loses the black line without regaining it by the next tile.
  • Lack of progress occurs when the robot reaches a line that is not in the intended sequence.

Transition & Victims

  • The transition from the field to evacuation zone is identified by a silver tape line across the entrance.
  • There are 3 live victims represented by silver balls.
  • There are 3 dead victims represented by black balls.

Evacuation Multipliers

  • Each team chooses their evacuation zone level.
  • The challenge is to save the 6 victims by placing them in the evacuation point.
  • Victims rescued provide multipliers on points accumulated from the line follow zone.
  • Multipliers vary, such as 1.0, 1.1, 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, and more depending on level and victims rescued.
