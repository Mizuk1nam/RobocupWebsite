Leagues & Rules

RoboCupJunior Canada challenges students to combine robotics, coding, problem solving, creativity, and teamwork through autonomous robot competitions.

General Competition Rules

Team & Robot Requirements

  • Teams compete in Primary or Secondary sub-leagues.
  • Teams must have 2 to 4 students and 1 teacher or mentor.
  • OnStage teams may include up to 5 students.
  • A team member or robot cannot be shared between teams.
  • Each team can compete in only one event.
  • Robots must be built and programmed by student team members.
  • Robots must operate autonomously; remote controls are not allowed.
  • Teams must ask the referee before touching an active robot.
  • Teams should arrive 5 minutes before their scheduled competition time.

Leagues

🧱

Maze

A RoboParty challenge where robots navigate a walled corridor from start to finish.

  • 3 attempts per robot.
  • 120 second maximum per run.
  • Wall contact can reduce the score.
View details
🏎

Formula 1

A RoboParty challenge where robots follow a black curved line around a race track.

  • 3 attempts per robot.
  • Best run is counted.
  • Fast and stable line-following is key.
View details
🎵

Performance

A RoboParty challenge where teams present a short robot routine with music, creativity, and technical design.

  • Usually a 1 minute routine.
  • Creativity and entertainment are judged.
  • Programming complexity matters.
View details
🤖

SumoBots

A RoboParty challenge where robots try to push opponents out of an octagonal ring.

  • Robots compete inside a ring.
  • Points are earned by pushing opponents out.
  • Size and weight limits apply.
View details
🚑

Rescue Line

Robots follow a black line through a rescue course while handling intersections, obstacles, gaps, speed bumps, and evacuation zones.

  • Points are awarded for completing course elements.
  • Robots may need to recover from gaps or obstacles.
  • The evacuation zone uses victim objects and point multipliers.
View details
🧭

Rescue Maze

Robots explore a simulated disaster maze to locate victims and deliver rescue kits while operating fully autonomously.

  • Designed as preparation for international Rescue divisions.
  • Teams may participate in Rescue Maze and Rescue Line at qualifying events.
View details
⚽

Soccer

Two autonomous robots compete against another pair by detecting and kicking a ball into the opponent's goal.

  • Lightweight uses an infrared ball.
  • Open uses a vision-tracked orange ball.
  • Games include two 10 minute halves and a 5 minute half-time.
  • Each goal is worth 1 point.
View details
🎭

OnStage

Teams create a robotic performance that combines engineering, programming, music, movement, storytelling, and design.

  • Robots and students perform together on stage.
  • Judges consider creativity, entertainment, and programming complexity.
  • Robot autonomy and sensor use are considered during evaluation.
